## I. Cosmology and Origin

### The Fabric of Aerus

Aerus is a spherical world suspended in the Starry Void. It is lit by two suns: **Kael**, the golden sun, and **Syr**, the pale sun. Their opposing cycles create full-bright days, double twilights, and nights of complete darkness.

Magic is not a separate supernatural force. It is the underlying fabric that stitches matter to the spiritual plane. The ancients called it the **Primordial Thread**.

Before the catastrophe, all life in Aerus interacted with magic naturally. Trees grew in perfect geometric patterns, stones sang at inaudible frequencies, and the Thread itself could be perceived by the gifted as rivers of light passing through mountains and seas.

### Vor'Athek, the Hungry

**Vor'Athek** is not a god in any conventional sense. It predates religion, culture, and perhaps language itself. Surviving Aeridian fragments describe it in mutually contradictory ways: an absence with shape, a silence that devours sound, a hunger without a mouth.

Roughly four thousand years ago, Vor'Athek touched the Primordial Thread. It did not break the Thread. It **tainted** it. From that moment onward, magic ceased to be trustworthy. Healing could cause necrosis. Light could draw shadows. Summoned fire could burn the soul as easily as flesh.

The Aeridians responded with the **Sealing Ritual**, the greatest magical act in recorded history. The ritual succeeded only partially. Vor'Athek was driven back, but the Aeridian civilization was destroyed in the process. The ritual's epicenter became the **Arcane Ash Desert**, and the Primordial Thread remained permanently corrupted.

### The Hidden Truth

Vor'Athek was not truly erased. It was trapped.

The deepest hidden truth in Aerus is that Vor'Athek is imprisoned **inside** the Primordial Thread itself. Magical Surges, failures, and distortions are not random accidents. They are pressure against a prison that was never completed.

Only the Guild of Threads understands this with any clarity, and even they lack the full picture.

## II. History - The Four Eras

### 1. Age of the Aeridians

The Aeridians ruled a single global civilization without national borders. They mastered the five elements and Spirit magic with extraordinary precision, built gravity-defying suspended cities, and organized themselves through conclaves rather than permanent monarchies.

The greatest of these assemblies, the **Plenary of the Thread**, met in **Aerum Primordial**, now buried beneath the Heart of Ashes.

The Sealing Ritual was not unanimously approved. A dissenting faction argued that using the Primordial Thread as part of the prison would doom the world later. They lost the vote, and history proved them right.

### 2. Age of Silence

After the ritual, the world collapsed. The suspended cities fell. Survivors scattered. Magic became unstable and deadly. Written history nearly vanished; this era survives mostly through bones, ruins, and fragmentary oral traditions.

One major artifact survived intact: the **Crystal of Silence**, preserved today in the Guild's vaults in Vel'Ossian. The Guild does not fully understand it, but treats it as critically dangerous.

### 3. Age of Reconstruction

Over the following millennia, new civilizations rose from Aeridian ruins:

- **Valdoria** treated magic as an instrument of state and control.
- **Shaleth** treated magic as language, art, and disciplined improvisation.
- **Estravar** treated magic as craft, metallurgy, and endurance.
- **Khorrath** treated magic as a dangerous inheritance best approached with caution.
- **Veth** developed rhythmic, movement-based magical traditions distinct from the rest of Aerus.

### 4. Age of Scars

The current age is dominated by old wounds rather than stable peace.

Two centuries ago, the **Empire of Valdrek** unified most of Valdoria by combining military force, political pressure, and access to **Aeridian Fragments**, relics that stabilize magic and remain immune to corruption.

Current year: **4217 Post-Sealing**.

The common calendar divides the year into three 90-day seasons:

- **High Kael**: long, hot days.
- **Crossing**: unstable weather and frequent double twilights.
- **High Syr**: cold, pale, shortened days.

## III. Geography

### Global Overview

Aerus is composed of five continents separated by oceans and, in key places, by **Void Zones** where the Thread is so damaged that normal life cannot endure.

The **Myr Ocean** functions as the diplomatic and commercial axis of the world. Whoever controls its routes influences trade, information, and power.

### Valdoria

Valdoria is the political center of the known world. Its climate is temperate, with fertile plains, strong rivers, and western mountains. The land contains veins of fossilized ether, which make magic slightly more stable than elsewhere.

Key powers and locations:

- **Empire of Valdrek**: dominant imperial power centered in **Auramveld**.
- **Principalities of Osthara**: defensive confederation resisting full imperial absorption.
- **Ferren March**: nominal vassal, practically occupied.
- **Calmveth**: mercantile republic and vital port.
- **Gorath Fissures**: canyons marked by local time distortions.
- **Ondrek Pass**: the only reliable land crossing toward Estravar.

### Shaleth

Shaleth is the eastern continent, humid and forested, with the ancestral woodland of **Vel'Arath** dominating much of its territory.

The Thread in Vel'Arath does not feel merely less corrupted. It feels **older**, as if Vor'Athek never fully touched it there.

Key locations:

- **Vel'Shar**: center of elven power.
- **Vel'Arath**: untamed ancestral forest.
- **Threshold of Vel'Arath**: liminal region where compasses fail and time seems distorted.

### Estravar

Estravar is the frozen north: arctic, mountainous, and defined by endurance.

Its most important resource is **Keth**, a mineral able to absorb and stabilize corrupted magic. Because of this, every major power depends on Estravar whether it admits it or not.

Key locations:

- **Kethara**: confederation tied to Keth extraction and trade.
- **Stenvaard**: militarized port and strategic fortress.
- **Pale Belt**: void-touched frontier separating Estravar from Valdoria.

### Khorrath

Khorrath is the southern ash desert and the spiritual wound of the world.

It contains the ritual epicenter, vast Aeridian ruins, and the most dangerous concentrations of magical instability still accessible to mortals.

Key locations:

- **Khor-Ath**: political center at the desert edge.
- **Wandering Cities**: mobile settlements following water and survival routes.
- **Heart of Ashes**: a 40 km crater at the center of the old catastrophe.
- **The Last Chamber**: an intact Aeridian structure hidden at the center of the Heart of Ashes.

### Veth

Veth lies far to the southeast and was affected less directly by the original shockwave. As a result, its magical culture evolved differently: more rhythmic, embodied, and ceremonial.

Key locations:

- **Veth-Ara**: primary known contact point.
- **Forbidden Interior**: region even locals avoid.
- **Red Sea**: unstable ocean between Veth and Khorrath.

### The Isles of Myr

The Isles of Myr are a neutral archipelago protected by the **Pact of Myr**. Their routes are too strategically important for any major power to be allowed total control.

The islands are administered by the **Myr Council**, a merchant oligarchy that survives by balancing every faction against the others.

The Thread in Myr is chaotic but not fully corrupted. Magic often produces harmless anomalies called **Bloomings**: light that sings, conjured water that tastes like honey, or rain with impossible color and flavor.

Major cities:

- **Port Myr**: commercial capital and campaign starting point.
- **Vel'Ossian**: headquarters of the Guild of Threads.
- **Sanctum**: headquarters of the Church of the Pure Flame.

## IV. Major Factions

### Church of the Pure Flame

Headquartered in Sanctum, the Church teaches that the corruption of the Thread was a necessary purification rather than a disaster.

Publicly, it presents itself as the guardian of permitted magic through the **Flame Seals**, a licensing system that defines lawful magical practice.

Privately, its founders knew Vor'Athek was real and that the corruption was no blessing. Doctrine became a tool for controlling access to magic.

### Empire of Valdrek

The Empire is the largest military and administrative power in Aerus. It uses Keth, Aeridian Fragments, bureaucracy, and coercive diplomacy to maintain its dominance.

Its greatest hidden vulnerability is that it relies on Fragments as magical infrastructure while its own ruler, **Valdek IV**, is bound to a corrupted fragment-linked condition tied to the worsening crisis.

### Guild of Threads

The Guild preserves magical knowledge, Aeridian texts, and advanced research. Officially it is a scholarly body. In practice it is also an intelligence network.

The Guild knows more than any other institution about the Thread's deterioration and the incomplete nature of the Sealing.

### Children of the Broken Thread

The Children are the most hunted faction in Aerus. They argue that forbidden Aeridian knowledge was suppressed not because it was inherently evil, but because institutions feared losing control.

Led loosely through the influence of **the Weaver**, they believe the Last Chamber holds the only path to restoring the Thread.

### Myr Council

The Myr Council is not ideologically grand, but it is indispensable. It governs the neutral trade islands, brokers illicit information, and keeps the world speaking even while it tears itself apart.

## V. The Dome and the Travelers

The **Dome** is an Aeridian emergency protocol activated when the Thread's degradation reaches a critical threshold. The current Travelers are not the first summoning attempt, only the latest successful one.

Travelers matter because they arrive **without a native Thread signature**. That makes them harder for Vor'Athek to sense and allows them to approach truths and locations that natives cannot reach safely.

Each Traveler bears a **Dome Mark** on the left forearm. It pulses near magic, resonates with other Travelers, and behaves strangely near Surges, Fragments, and sites of Traveler death.

The Dome's real goal is not simply to observe. It wants a Traveler to reach the Last Chamber and act at the decisive point of the unfinished Sealing.

## VI. Prophecy and Endgame Pressure

Many factions know fragments of a prophecy tied to the Last Chamber, but no one agrees on its meaning.

- The **Church** claims such prophecy is fabricated heresy.
- The **Guild** sees it as a coded warning about Vor'Athek's prison.
- The **Children** interpret it as proof that the cure lies in forbidden restoration.
- The **Empire** treats prophecy as secondary to control of the outcome.

The crucial fact is this: every major faction points toward the Last Chamber, but each wants something incompatible from it.

## VII. Core World Principles for Play

- Magic is powerful, unstable, and never morally neutral.
- Death matters. Survival has cost.
- Institutions lie, but usually for reasons rooted in fear or control rather than simple villainy.
- The world is already in motion before the players arrive.
- The Travelers are not chosen because they are pure. They are useful because they are outsiders.
- There is no universally safe ending for Aerus. Every solution has consequence.
