# Aerus RPG - Formal Class Mutations

> Mutation milestones for the Aerus class system at levels 25, 50, 75, and 100.

---

## Purpose

Formal mutations convert organic growth into durable class identity. They are larger than routine every-five-level progression and should feel like visible transformations in how a character fights, survives, or changes the fiction around them.

---

## Mutation Framework

### Level 25

The first major branch. The character becomes legible as a specialist.

Expected outcomes:

- one defining active skill
- one reinforced passive
- a clearer tactical role

### Level 50

Convergence. Earlier choices begin to merge.

Expected outcomes:

- hybrid ability design
- reduced friction between previous paths
- stronger class signature

### Level 75

Ascended threshold. The character begins to operate at setting-significant scale.

Expected outcomes:

- abilities that shape scenes, not only turns
- rare or restricted powers
- stronger narrative visibility

### Level 100

Singular identity. This should feel legendary, not merely optimized.

Expected outcomes:

- one near-irreproducible power
- clear narrative consequence for use
- strong symbolic connection to the character's history

---

## Mutation Guidelines By Class

### Blade

Themes:

- stance mastery
- counter-pressure
- disciplined violence
- endurance through repetition

High-level mutation examples:

- ignore layered defenses for one decisive strike
- protect allies by intercepting lethal intent
- transform damage taken into tactical advantage

### Sorcerer

Themes:

- elemental specialization
- dangerous amplification
- ritual collapse and control
- spell interaction mastery

High-level mutation examples:

- dual-cast under extreme cost
- fuse incompatible elements
- reshape a local magical field

### Sharpshooter

Themes:

- line-of-sight dominance
- predictive targeting
- battlefield setup
- pressure through distance

High-level mutation examples:

- bend shots around cover
- trigger chained trap detonations
- transform an ally's fall into a decisive counterattack

### Shadow

Themes:

- invisibility of intent
- identity theft
- perfect infiltration
- burst lethality through preparation

High-level mutation examples:

- become undetectable for a short decisive window
- weaponize observation and memory
- assume another identity with near-perfect fidelity

### Herald

Themes:

- command presence
- morale shaping
- ideological force
- destiny through speech

High-level mutation examples:

- restore allied resolve without healing
- break enemy formation through declaration alone
- bind a vow that changes future consequences

### Sentinel

Themes:

- immovability
- denial zones
- ally protection
- punishment for overextension

High-level mutation examples:

- create a temporary no-passage zone
- absorb what should kill another character
- harden against corruption or ritual backlash

### Channeler

Themes:

- resonance with the Thread
- ritual channeling
- spirit or memory mediation
- dangerous overexposure

High-level mutation examples:

- stabilize a corrupted magical zone briefly
- call memory into combat relevance
- trade bodily safety for overwhelming channel output

### Wanderer

Themes:

- adaptation
- route mastery
- survival ingenuity
- growth through movement

High-level mutation examples:

- convert terrain into immediate advantage
- find safe passage where maps fail
- draw power from travel history and lived scars

---

## Design Constraints

- Mutations must reinforce the class fantasy.
- The more world-shaping a mutation is, the less freely it should be used.
- High-tier powers should be memorable enough to justify documentation in campaign history.
- No mutation should erase the need for teamwork.
