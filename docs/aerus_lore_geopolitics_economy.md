## XI. Geopolitics and Active Tensions

### Tension 1 - HOT: Empire vs. the Principalities of Osthara

The cold war is close to becoming an open one. The Principalities expect to move for independence after the death of Valdek IV. The Empire has been shifting troops under the excuse of "border exercises" for the last six months. The Principalities, meanwhile, have been purchasing Keth in volumes that make sense only for rearmament.

**Impact on Travelers:** Any land route across Valdoria crosses a zone of rising military tension.

### Tension 2 - COLD: Church vs. Guild of Threads

The Church and the Guild are locked in a permanent information war. The Church suppresses texts that undermine doctrine. The Guild preserves copies. Three years ago, a Church operative was found dead inside the Guild archives, and the Guild claims not to know how it happened.

**Impact on Travelers:** Being identified as an ally of one faction creates automatic hostility from the other.

### Tension 3 - STRUCTURAL: Kethara vs. Everyone Else

Kethara controls Ondrek Pass and the most important Keth deposits. Everyone needs Keth. The Empire tracks every shipment. Kethara knows it is being watched and raises prices accordingly. It is stable mutual extortion.

### Tension 4 - NEW: Children of the Broken Thread vs. the Order of Cinders

The Children are trying to reach the Heart of Ashes. The Order of Cinders sees that as an existential threat. Two violent clashes in the last year have made Khorrath even more volatile.

**Impact on Travelers:** Khorrath is increasingly militarized by a religious faction that treats outsiders as immediate suspects.

## XVI. Recent Events (4212-4217 PS)

This section records the six most relevant events of the last five years for rumor tables, NPC conversations, and the campaign's opening political atmosphere.

### Event 1 - The Great Keth Spike (4212 PS)

**What happened:** The price of raw Keth tripled in under four months. Kethara merchants reported unprecedented volume purchases from the Principalities of Osthara. The Empire of Valdrek investigated and officially filed the matter away as speculative demand.

**What really happened:** Osthara is secretly rearming its garrisons with Keth-stabilized equipment: armor, weapons, and projectiles. The goal is to field troops that can operate inside Surge zones without the same disadvantages suffered by standard imperial forces.

**Who knows what:**

- **Empire of Valdrek:** Saw surface evidence but chose not to conclude anything. The real analysis was buried by a senior official who may already be compromised.
- **Guild of Threads:** Knows the purchase volumes and is watching without intervening.
- **Children of the Broken Thread:** Know and consider it welcome news, because anything that weakens the Empire is useful to them.
- **Church of the Pure Flame:** Largely unaware.

**Port Myr NPC:** **Maren Toss** saw Ostharan ships arriving heavy and leaving empty through Pier Seven. For 20 gold marks, he can provide names and dates.  
*"They buy stone. A lot of stone. And they are no miners."*

**Player hook:** A Kethara merchant arrives in Port Myr in panic after his Ostharan contract was abruptly canceled when he asked too many questions about the cargo destination. Someone is following him.

### Event 2 - The Third Floor of Vel'Ossian Was Sealed (4213 PS)

**What happened:** The Guild announced "structural repairs" on the third floor of its headquarters in Vel'Ossian and closed it indefinitely. Scholars were reassigned without explanation.

**What really happened:** A group of researchers discovered hidden Aeridian documents behind the north wall. Those documents describe the Sealing Ritual in unprecedented detail, including the line no senior member wants spoken aloud:  
*"The Sealing was never permanent. It was designed to last four thousand years."*

**Who knows what:**

- **Guild of Threads:** Leadership knows and is in internal emergency deliberation. The information is classified at conclave level.
- **Empire of Valdrek:** Does not know the content, but agent **Caden Orryl** is investigating the Guild's abnormal behavior.
- **Church:** Does not know. If it did, it would use the discovery as proof that the Guild hides sacred truths.
- **Children of the Broken Thread:** Thresh knows the Guild found something, but not what.

**Port Myr NPC:** **Caden Orryl**, posing as a merchant's secretary at the Five Flags Docks. He knows the third floor was closed and will pay 40 gold marks for a specific reason.

**Player hook:** A former researcher relocated from Vel'Ossian is drinking himself numb in Port Myr. He mutters about walls that should not exist and truths the ancients already knew. His memory was wiped by the Guild.

### Event 3 - The Last Public Appearance of Valdek IV (4214 PS)

**What happened:** Emperor Valdek IV took part in the Procession of the Eternal Flame in 4214 PS and has not been seen in public since. Imperial decrees continue to be issued under his seal. Audiences were suspended "for health reasons."

**What really happened:** Valdek IV is being kept alive beyond his natural limit by a corrupted Aeridian Fragment implanted in his chest during a covert medical rite in 4213. He knows. He agreed. He believes the decay of his body is a fair price for imperial stability.

**Who knows what:**

- **Empire of Valdrek:** Knows the emperor is ill, but not the full truth. Three internal blocs are already competing for the future regency.
- **Guild of Threads:** Suspects a fragment is involved, but lacks confirmation.
- **Church of the Pure Flame:** Publicly prays for the emperor's recovery while privately discussing succession options.
- **Children of the Broken Thread:** Know about the corrupted Fragment and treat it as proof of imperial hypocrisy.

**Port Myr NPC:** **Thresh** has an intercepted letter from an Auramveld noble describing "the smell of ash in the emperor's chamber." For 15 gold marks and minimum Children reputation, he shares it.

**Player hook:** An imperial courier arrives in Port Myr carrying a sealed package meant for a discreet apothecary. The package contains refined Keth extract. The courier dies before delivery. Who was meant to receive it, and why?

### Event 4 - The Two Clashes (4215 PS)

**What happened:** Two violent clashes erupted between members of the Children of the Broken Thread and the newly formed **Order of Cinders**, a pro-imperial militia with strong hardline Church sympathies. The first clash was in Vel'Ossian. The second was in Kethara.

**What really happened:** The Order of Cinders is not spontaneous. It is backed by a cluster of imperial officers who want the Children destroyed without involving the regular army directly. Both confrontations were deliberately engineered.

**Who knows what:**

- **Empire of Valdrek:** Officially condemns both sides as agitators. Privately, some of its backers are satisfied.
- **Children of the Broken Thread:** Know they were trapped, but not exactly by whom.
- **Church of the Pure Flame:** Publicly condemns the Children. Hardline clergy sympathize with the Order.
- **Guild of Threads:** Watching and deeply concerned by escalation.

**Port Myr NPC:** **Thresh** lost a personal contact in the Vel'Ossian clash. He can be pushed toward radicalism unless the players give him another path.

**Player hook:** A former member of the Order of Cinders has fled to Port Myr in remorse. He knows the names of the imperial officers who financed the operation. His former comrades are already hunting him.

### Event 5 - The Three Ships from Shaleth (4216 PS)

**What happened:** Three ships from Shaleth docked at Pier Seven in Port Myr in 4216 PS. Their declared cargo was routine ballast and return supplies. The Myr Council accepted the paperwork without inspection.

**What really happened:** The ships carried a Guild cell that did not want to be tracked by land. The real cargo, unloaded at midnight, was a series of sealed crates containing Crystals of Silence and field research equipment. The Myr Council was bribed not to record the delivery.

**Who knows what:**

- **Myr Council:** Knows it accepted the bribe, but not the exact contents.
- **Guild of Threads:** Knows it was an internal operation. The final destination of the researchers is restricted even within the Guild.
- **Empire of Valdrek:** Caden Orryl noticed the ships but was blocked from inspection.
- **Church of the Pure Flame:** Did not notice.

**Port Myr NPC:** **Maren Toss** witnessed the midnight unloading. For 20 gold marks he describes the boxes, the unfamiliar seal, and the fact that the crew spoke Elven among themselves.

**Player hook:** One crate was left behind in a warehouse near Pier Seven. Forgotten, or abandoned on purpose. Maren never opened it.

### Event 6 - The Leaked Kael Archive Report (4217 PS)

**What happened:** A classified report from the Kael Archive was leaked. It concludes that the Sealing is degrading by roughly **3% per year**. Under conservative projections, collapse comes in under 34 years. Under optimistic ones, perhaps 60.

**What really happened:** The report confirmed what the Guild had already begun to suspect after the Vel'Ossian discovery. Someone inside the Archive decided the Empire could not keep the truth to itself any longer and leaked it to multiple recipients, likely including Caden Orryl and, possibly, the Weaver.

**Who knows what:**

- **Kael Archive / Empire:** Knows the leak is real and treats it as an active security crisis.
- **Guild of Threads:** Received the report and recognizes it as confirmation.
- **Children of the Broken Thread:** Thresh received only a fragment, but enough to know time is running out.
- **Church of the Pure Flame:** Has not received it yet. Its reaction would be unpredictable.
- **Myr Council:** Still unaware.

**Port Myr NPC:** **Maren Toss** saw the Dome light the sky over Port Myr for three seconds on the day the Travelers arrived. He recorded the date, time, and passenger names and is waiting for someone to ask.  
*"You arrived the same day the sky lit up. That is not coincidence. There is no coincidence in Aerus."*

**Player hook:** Caden Orryl has the full report and is considering placing it in the Travelers' hands, but only if he becomes convinced they are not already controlled by one of the factions.

## XVII. Regional Economy

### Currency System

Aerus uses four common coin denominations:

| Currency | Symbol | Equivalence |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Copper Shards | CS | Base unit |
| Silver Wheels | SW | 1 SW = 10 CS |
| Gold Marks | GM | 1 GM = 20 SW = 200 CS |
| Platinum Discs | PD | 1 PD = 50 GM = 1,000 SW |

Regional notes:

- Copper Shards are universal but cumbersome.
- Silver Wheels are the most common practical currency for travelers and artisans.
- Gold Marks circulate among institutions, nobility, and merchants.
- Platinum Discs are rare outside high-value contracts and property deals.

### Why Prices Differ

- **Port Myr:** Port demand and contraband pressure inflate many prices, but locally produced potions remain competitive.
- **Auramveld:** Imperial bureaucracy and taxation raise costs.
- **Vel'Ossian:** Balanced market, high quality, best place for arcane goods.
- **Kethara:** Cheap shelter and food, but imported goods cost more.
- **Khorrath:** Lower bureaucracy, lower trust, and more barter-driven trade.

### Keth as a Strategic Resource

Keth is extracted almost exclusively from the mines of Estravar under Kethara control. It is strategically vital because:

- It is the only natural mineral known to stabilize corrupted magic reliably.
- Keth-infused equipment functions in Surge zones where ordinary magic fails.
- There is no viable synthetic substitute on the open market.

Imperial control measures include:

- Registry offices in major ports, including Port Myr.
- Mandatory declaration for shipments above 5 kg.
- Imperial licensing for purchases above 50 kg.
- Confiscation, fines, and imprisonment for serious violations.

### Gray Market in Port Myr

- Undeclared Keth often enters disguised as construction material or mixed mineral cargo.
- The Myr Council applies the same policy it uses elsewhere: selective blindness in exchange for informal contribution.
- **Seyla Vorn** is one of the few vendors willing to move raw Keth without formal paperwork.
- Gray market benchmark: Grade 1 Keth at 6 GM/kg instead of the legal 8 GM/kg.

### Trade Routes

**Port Myr**
- Exports: salted fish, island amber, information, smuggled goods, port services.
- Imports: almost everything. It is the world's redistribution harbor.

**Auramveld**
- Exports: bureaucracy, licensed mercenaries, official seals, industrial production.
- Imports: Keth, food, arcane craft goods.

**Vel'Ossian**
- Exports: knowledge, scrolls, artifacts, Guild services, academic formation.
- Imports: Keth, food, laboratory materials.

**Kethara**
- Exports: Keth in all grades, building stone, secondary minerals.
- Imports: processed food, textiles, tools, potions.
