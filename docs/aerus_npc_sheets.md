# Aerus RPG - Expanded NPC Sheets

> Reference sheets for major NPCs, including role, faction alignment, initial posture, and narrative function.

---

## How To Use This Document

These sheets are not meant to turn every major NPC into a combat encounter. Their main purpose is to preserve consistency in voice, leverage, secrets, and campaign utility.

Each NPC entry should answer:

- where they are most likely to appear
- who they serve
- how they initially treat the party
- what secret pressure they carry
- what narrative function they fulfill

---

## Valdek IV - The Emperor

### Profile

- Location: Auramveld, with influence reaching Port Myr through imperial agents
- Faction: `empire_valdrek`
- Initial posture: indifferent until the Travelers become relevant

### Narrative Function

Valdek IV is not a standard antagonist and should rarely be framed as a direct encounter. He represents empire as infrastructure: order, expansion, fear, logistics, and political inevitability.

### Secret Pressure

His rule is sustained by power that is also slowly destroying him. The same force helping him preserve imperial stability may be worsening magical instability around the capital.

### Use Cases

- distant patron
- political threat
- succession crisis anchor
- symbol of lawful decay

## The Weaver

### Profile

- Location: itinerant, usually near major Thread instability
- Faction: historically tied to `guild_of_threads`, but effectively independent
- Initial posture: guarded, distant, and difficult to read

### Narrative Function

The Weaver is an oracle-adjacent figure. She should appear when the campaign narrows, not when the players merely ask for exposition. She knows too much, reveals too little, and is never fully trustworthy in a comfortable way.

### Secret Pressure

She knows more than almost anyone about the sealing problem, but believes the obvious solution may only delay catastrophe.

### Use Cases

- late-arc guide
- ambiguity engine
- reality-check after major decisions
- keeper of dangerous truth

## Maren Toss - Gatekeeper Of Pier Seven

### Profile

- Location: Port Myr, Pier Seven
- Faction: informally connected to `myr_council`
- Initial posture: mildly favorable if he saw the Travelers arrive

### Narrative Function

Maren is a grounded local information broker. He is useful because he is ordinary, observant, and patient. He gives the setting social texture and helps the city feel lived in.

### Secret Pressure

He has tracked strange dome-related anomalies for years without understanding them. The Travelers may be the first people who make those records matter.

### Use Cases

- ship and cargo records
- rumor gate
- early-game trust anchor
- practical city intelligence

## Seyla Vorn - Master Alchemist

### Profile

- Location: Port Myr, Broken Square
- Faction: independent
- Initial posture: transactional before personal

### Narrative Function

Seyla is the party's natural entry point into crafting, ingredient logic, and practical magical investigation. She is valuable because she links commerce, experimentation, and buried structural danger.

### Secret Pressure

She has detected a pattern between local anomalies and larger magical surges, but she does not yet know who can be trusted with that information.

### Use Cases

- crafting onboarding
- reagent access
- anomaly research
- mid-game investigative ally

---

## Caden Orryl - Imperial Intermediary

### Profile

- Location: usually on assignment between imperial and foreign power centers
- Faction: `empire_valdrek`
- Initial posture: polite, strategic, never naive

### Narrative Function

Caden is the empire in conversational form. He should be persuasive, practical, and dangerous because he makes compromise sound reasonable.

### Secret Pressure

He may be preparing for an imperial future beyond Valdek IV and could treat the Travelers as assets before deciding whether they are liabilities.

### Use Cases

- soft-power recruiter
- diplomatic threat
- succession politics
- "civilized" face of empire

---

## Custodian Figures Of The Guild Of Threads

### Profile

- Location: archives, observatories, magical institutions, and controlled study zones
- Faction: `guild_of_threads`
- Initial posture: cautious curiosity

### Narrative Function

Guild custodians should represent disciplined knowledge, not heroic certainty. They know more than most people, but not enough to control what is coming.

### Secret Pressure

Their institutions may already understand that the world is degrading faster than public stability can survive.

### Use Cases

- knowledge gatekeepers
- lore verification
- mission sponsors
- conflict point between truth and containment

---

## Church Operatives

### Profile

- Location: sanctums, patrols, inquisitorial routes, purification sites
- Faction: `church_pure_flame`
- Initial posture: suspicious unless legitimacy is proven

### Narrative Function

Church operatives should feel morally serious, not cartoonishly cruel. They believe purification is necessary, and that belief is what makes them dangerous.

### Secret Pressure

Some already suspect that orthodox doctrine is insufficient for what is coming, but they cannot admit that without destabilizing the institution itself.

### Use Cases

- investigation pressure
- moral confrontation
- social obstacle
- uneasy alliance against corruption

---

## Children Of The Broken Thread Contacts

### Profile

- Location: hidden cells, forbidden ruins, unstable magical edges
- Faction: `children_of_broken_thread`
- Initial posture: probing, ideological, wary

### Narrative Function

These contacts should present the strongest challenge to official narratives. They are not automatically correct, but they are willing to look where others refuse.

### Secret Pressure

They may be closer to the truth than anyone else, but their methods can produce the very disasters they claim to prevent.

### Use Cases

- forbidden knowledge access
- faction recruitment pressure
- dangerous collaboration
- alternate endgame logic

---

## NPC Design Rules

- Every major NPC should want something before the party arrives.
- Power should come from position and knowledge, not only stats.
- Secrets should create future leverage, not just trivia.
- If an NPC matters politically, treat combat against them as campaign-shaping.
