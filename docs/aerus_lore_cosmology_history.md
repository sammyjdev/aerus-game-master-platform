## I. Cosmology and Origin

### The Fabric of Aerus

Aerus is a spherical world suspended in the Starry Void. It has two suns, Kael the golden and Syr the pale, which move in opposite cycles and create full-light days, double twilights, and nights of total darkness. Magic is not a supernatural force. It is the very fabric that stitches matter to the spiritual plane, known to the ancients as the **Primordial Thread**.

Before the catastrophe, every living thing in Aerus breathed magic the way it breathes air. Trees grew in perfect geometric patterns. Stones sang at frequencies no human ear could hear. Sensitive individuals could see the Primordial Thread as lines of light crossing mountains and seas.

### The Entity - Vor'Athek, the Hungry

**Vor'Athek** is not a god of any pantheon. It predates language. The surviving fragmented texts describe it in contradictory ways: an absence with shape, a silence that devours sound, a hunger without a mouth.

Roughly four thousand years ago, Vor'Athek touched the Primordial Thread for the first time. It did not break it. It **tasted** it. That interaction alone corrupted magic at its root. The Thread did not snap. It **twisted**. Healing magic gained a chance of necrosis. Invocations of light could attract shadows.

The Aeridians tried to drive Vor'Athek away through the **Sealing Ritual**, the greatest magical act ever attempted in Aerus. It worked only partially. Vor'Athek was pushed back into the Void, but the cost was the destruction of the civilization that performed the ritual.

**What the factions do not fully know:** Vor'Athek is imprisoned *inside* the Primordial Thread. Every magical Surge is it pushing outward. The Sealing was incomplete. Only the Guild of Threads knows this, and it keeps that knowledge buried.

## II. History - The Four Eras

### Age of the Aeridians

The Aeridians were a single global civilization without national borders. They mastered all five elements and Spirit magic with surgical precision. They built the **Suspended Cities**, structures that defied gravity through direct interaction with the Primordial Thread. There was no war, only debate.

They had no single central state. Instead, they relied on **Conclaves**, regional assemblies that governed by consensus. The greatest of them, the **Plenary of the Thread**, gathered representatives from the whole world once each generation in **Aerum Primordial**, whose ruins now lie beneath the Heart of Ashes.

The Sealing Ritual was not a unanimous decision. One faction argued that using the Thread to seal Vor'Athek was like using poison as an antidote. They lost by seven votes. Their leaders were the last to survive the ritual, long enough to realize they had been right.

### Age of Silence

The world collapsed. Survivors scattered. Magic became unstable and lethal. The Suspended Cities fell. There are no written records from this era, only cave paintings and bones.

One artifact survived intact: the **Crystal of Silence**, kept in the Guild's vaults in Vel'Ossian. It is opaque, unbreakable, and emits a low-frequency sound that humans do not hear but animals avoid. The Guild does not know exactly what it is. It only knows the vault should never remain open for long.

### Age of Reconstruction

New civilizations emerged from Aeridian ruins. Without a shared memory of the past, each continent developed its own relationship with magic:

- **Valdoria:** magic as a tool of state, licensed and regulated.
- **Shaleth:** magic as language, where improvisation matters more than precision.
- **Estravar:** magic as craft, with enchantments designed to last decades within Keth.
- **Khorrath:** magic as a dangerous inheritance, used as little as possible.
- **Veth:** magic as rhythm, based on movement and sound rather than symbols.

### Current Age - The Age of Scars

The **Empire of Valdrek** unified the central continent two centuries ago thanks to **Aeridian Fragments**, pre-catastrophe artifacts immune to corruption.

Current year: **4217 Post-Sealing**.

The Ash Calendar divides the year into three 90-day seasons:

- **High Kael** (days 1-90): summer, long and hot days.
- **Crossing** (days 91-180): unstable weather and frequent double twilights.
- **High Syr** (days 181-270): winter, short days and pale light.
