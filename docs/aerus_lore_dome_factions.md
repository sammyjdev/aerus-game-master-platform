## IV. Major Factions

### 1. Church of the Pure Flame

**Headquarters:** Sanctum  
**Motto:** *"The Thread was not corrupted. It was purified."*

The Church teaches that corruption in the Thread is a blessing in disguise. In practice, it defines "permitted magic" through the **Flame Seals**, a licensing system it sells for profit. It is immensely wealthy and politically aligned with the Empire.

**What it hides:** Its founders knew Vor'Athek was real and that the Thread had been actively corrupted. The doctrine was built deliberately as a system of control.

### 2. Empire of Valdrek

**Headquarters:** Auramveld  
**Motto:** *"From Ash, Order."*

The largest military and economic power in Aerus. Its strength rests on three pillars: Keth-equipped armies, control of Aeridian Fragments, and an efficient bureaucracy.

Emperor **Valdek IV** is 67 years old and has no universally accepted legitimate heir.

**What it hides:** The Fragments are not merely relics. They are **anchors of the Thread**. The Empire uses them as infrastructure to keep Valdoria habitable. The **Kael Archive** tracks the Thread's deterioration in silence, and its numbers are worse than almost anyone suspects.

### 3. Guild of Threads

**Headquarters:** Vel'Ossian  
**Motto:** *"Knowledge chooses no side."*

The Guild catalogs and preserves magical knowledge. It is the greatest living repository of Aeridian texts.

**What it hides:** It knows Vor'Athek is inside the Thread. It works quietly to prevent that prison from failing. It also keeps records of every Traveler summoning from the past two hundred years.

### 4. Children of the Broken Thread

**Headquarters:** Mobile / Nomadic  
**Motto:** *"The Thread needs no anchors. It needs freedom."*

They are led by **the Weaver**, whose identity remains unknown. The Children believe the Last Chamber contains the cure for the Thread.

**What they hide:** The Weaver is a former Guild researcher expelled for trying to access classified archives. She discovered that the Thread is actively deteriorating and will collapse completely in roughly two hundred years without intervention. She knows the Last Chamber is the only possible solution, but does not know it responds to intent.

## V. The Dome

### Nature

The Dome is an Aeridian emergency protocol activated when the Thread reaches a critical deterioration threshold. That threshold was crossed eleven years ago. The first three summonings failed. The fourth produced the current Travelers.

### Why Travelers

Natives are born carrying an imprint within the corrupted Thread, which means Vor'Athek can sense them approaching. Travelers arrive **without a Thread signature**. To Vor'Athek, they are effectively invisible.

### The Dome Mark

An arcane tattoo on the left forearm. It appears as pale blue lines that pulse in the presence of active magic. Its known properties:

- It cannot be permanently removed. It reforms at the next dawn.
- It pulses more intensely near Aeridian Fragments.
- It pulses irregularly near Void Zones and Surges.
- Different Travelers' marks resonate with one another within 10 meters.
- In places where Travelers died, it turns cold rather than fading out.

Travelers may choose to conceal it with sleeves, gloves, or bandages.

### Communication

- **Dreams:** always in the Traveler's native language, always fragmented, always centered on an image of a sphere of light-thread suspended over an ash desert.
- **The Mark:** anomalous behavior in the mark itself works as passive communication.
- **Calculated Silence:** in moments of crisis, the mark may heat, pulse once, and do nothing else. The Dome seems to consider crisis part of the process.

### The Real Objective

The Dome wants a Traveler to reach the Last Chamber and touch the crystal with a genuine intention to seal Vor'Athek. A path back to the Traveler's original world does exist, but it only opens after the Sealing is completed. The Dome omitted that fact from the briefing.

### What the Factions Know

| Faction | Knowledge of the Dome |
| --- | --- |
| Church | Sees it as a divine instrument of purification and does not know its true objective |
| Empire | The Kael Archive has records of two centuries of summonings and knows the protocol is Aeridian |
| Guild | Knows more than anyone else, suspects the real objective, and has studied the mark for decades |
| Children | Believe the Dome and the Weaver want the same thing, but they are mistaken |
| Order of Cinders | Treats the Dome as the greatest active threat to the Heart of Ashes |
