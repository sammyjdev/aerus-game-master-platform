# Aerus RPG - Base Classes And Progression

> Core class identities, progression logic, and mutation philosophy for Aerus.

---

## Design Principle

Characters do not begin as rigid MMO-style builds. Level 1 establishes background pressure, instinct, and a first combat identity. Class growth is organic and responds to action, history, and narrative direction.

Progression model:

- Level 1: innate passive plus base skill
- Every 5 levels: organic advancement granted by the GM
- Formal mutations: levels 25, 50, 75, and 100

---

## Class List

The current base class set is:

1. Blade
2. Sorcerer
3. Sharpshooter
4. Shadow
5. Herald
6. Sentinel
7. Channeler
8. Wanderer

These names are intentionally broad. They describe a combat and narrative posture, not a rigid job label.

---

## 1. Blade

### Fantasy

Front-line martial pressure, discipline, endurance, and precision under threat.

### Innate Passive

`Muscle Memory`

Repeated exposure to the same type of damage gradually improves resistance during the encounter.

### Base Skill

`Combat Stance`

Choose an offensive or defensive posture at the start of the turn.

### Common Mutation Paths

- Guardian: protection, interception, defense scaling
- Executioner: bleed, finishing pressure, aggression
- Duelist: single-target focus, counterplay, tempo control

---

## 2. Sorcerer

### Fantasy

Elemental or arcane control, unstable magical force, and mastery under corruption risk.

### Innate Passive

`Instinctive Affinity`

The primary element is easier to channel safely.

### Base Skill

`Focused Casting`

Prepare power for the next spell to increase effect or reliability.

### Common Mutation Paths

- Alchemist: unstable combinations and fusion
- Specialist: stronger elemental focus
- Occultist: spirit-adjacent or forbidden manipulation

---

## 3. Sharpshooter

### Fantasy

Distance control, target selection, battlefield pressure, and opportunistic precision.

### Innate Passive

`Predator's Eye`

The first strike of a fight lands with extra lethality.

### Base Skill

`Calibrated Aim`

Reduces range penalties and improves shot reliability.

### Common Mutation Paths

- Hunter: tracking and marked-target pressure
- Arcane Marksman: infused projectiles
- Artillery: area pressure and multi-target coverage

---

## 4. Shadow

### Fantasy

Information advantage, infiltration, controlled deception, and surgical violence.

### Innate Passive

`Environmental Reading`

Rapidly identifies exits, valuables, and visible security pressure in a new location.

### Base Skill

`Silent Angle`

Improves stealth positioning, approach control, or first-strike advantage depending on the scene.

### Common Mutation Paths

- Infiltrator: movement and detection evasion
- Assassin: burst damage and vital strikes
- Illusionist: decoys, distortion, and layered lies

---

## 5. Herald

### Fantasy

Presence, conviction, command, and emotionally charged influence over allies or enemies.

### Innate Passive

`Weight Of Voice`

Words carry unusual force when the character speaks with intent.

### Base Skill

`Command Pulse`

Reinforces allies, pressures weak resolve, or shifts momentum through presence rather than raw damage.

### Common Mutation Paths

- Commander: formation and party tempo
- Zealot: conviction through sacrifice
- Oracle-Herald: prophecy, omen, destabilizing truth

---

## 6. Sentinel

### Fantasy

Durability, positional control, stubborn protection, and battlefield denial.

### Innate Passive

`Hold The Line`

The Sentinel becomes harder to displace once firmly planted.

### Base Skill

`Guard Breaker / Guard Wall`

The class pivots between stopping movement and shielding allies.

### Common Mutation Paths

- Bastion: pure defense and anchoring
- Warden: control and punishment
- Iron Saint: sacrifice, endurance, anti-corruption resilience

---

## 7. Channeler

### Fantasy

A conduit for forces larger than the self: ritual energy, memory, spirit, or ancient structure.

### Innate Passive

`Resonance`

The character senses instability and reacts more strongly to magical environments.

### Base Skill

`Open Channel`

Temporarily aligns the self with a source of power for increased effect and increased risk.

### Common Mutation Paths

- Ritualist: setup power and preparation
- Thread Adept: direct interaction with the Thread
- Spirit Medium: memory, omen, or dead-adjacent contact

---

## 8. Wanderer

### Fantasy

Adaptation, survival, multi-role competence, and unusual growth patterns shaped by travel.

### Innate Passive

`Survivor's Instinct`

The Wanderer gains small but meaningful advantages when operating outside stable civilization.

### Base Skill

`Improvised Response`

Turns terrain, inventory, or timing into flexible tactical value.

### Common Mutation Paths

- Pathfinder: movement and route mastery
- Scavenger: item improvisation and extraction
- Horizon-Bound: destiny, omen, and traveler-specific evolution

---

## Progression Philosophy

Class progression should never feel detached from play history. Mutations are not only upgrades; they are evidence of what the character has become.

Good mutation design:

- reflects actual player behavior
- reinforces a clear fantasy
- opens new tactical choices
- creates a cost, risk, or identity shift

Bad mutation design:

- generic numerical inflation
- disconnected power spikes
- choices that do not affect scene behavior

---

## Formal Mutation Milestones

### Level 25

The character commits to a more visible path.

### Level 50

Convergence begins. Skills blend across earlier choices and become more identity-defining.

### Level 75

Ascended play. Powers become rare, memorable, and narrative in consequence.

### Level 100

Singular mastery. The character acquires something difficult or impossible to replicate.

---

## GM Guidance

- Mutations should reward patterns the player already demonstrated.
- The best class identity is discovered, not imposed.
- Backstory matters, but action at the table matters more.
- If a power would flatten tension, attach cost, scarcity, or fallout.
