## Main Narrative Arcs

### Arc I - The Flame That Controls

**Faction:** Church of the Pure Flame

**Inciting incident:** A Church novice desperately approaches the players at the harbor with a partially burned manuscript. He recognized the Dome mark on them and believes they are signs of Purification. The manuscript contains records that contradict official doctrine.

**Objective 1:** Discover what the Church truly knows about the Thread.  
Via `church_01` and `church_02`.

**Decision point A:** Do the players hand over the manuscript or use it as leverage?

**Objective 2:** Understand what the Purification ritual actually does.  
Via `church_03` or an alternate infiltration path.

**Decision point B:** Do the players expose the practice or cover it up in exchange for access to the Last Chamber?

**Objective 3:** Confront High Purifier Cassiel with the evidence.

**Reveal:** The Church knows the Last Chamber exists. The doctrine of the Seals was created specifically to ensure that nobody without Church approval could reach it. The original founder of the Church was one of the dissidents from the Sealing Ritual, from the group that believed using the Thread to seal Vor'Athek was a mistake. They won the theological war later by rewriting history.

**Convergence:** The Church has a partial map of the Last Chamber. They need players to complete it, unless the players steal it first.

### Arc II - The Order That Collapses

**Faction:** Empire of Valdrek

**Inciting incident:** Caden Orryl approaches the players on the morning after their arrival with information they need to know now: the report on the degradation of the Sealing. He asks for nothing yet. He only wants them informed.

**Objective 1:** Understand the scale of the crisis and what the Kael Archive knows.  
Via `valdrek_01` and `valdrek_02`.

**Decision point A:** Do the players work for the Archive or use the information independently?

**Objective 2:** Discover that Valdek IV is part of the problem, because the corrupted Fragment sustaining him accelerates the collapse.  
Via `valdrek_03`.

**Decision point B:** Do the players tell the Empire about Valdek's connection to the degradation, or keep that information for themselves?

**Objective 3:** Reach Auramveld and find Mira Voss, who holds the Archive's version of the Last Chamber map.

**Reveal:** The Kael Archive calculated that if Valdek IV dies now, the Fragment inside him will destabilize catastrophically, guaranteeing a Surge within a 200 km radius. If he stays alive, the degradation keeps accelerating. There is no good political solution. The only possible solution lies through the Last Chamber.

**Convergence:** The Archive offers escort and resources for the Last Chamber expedition in exchange for access to whatever the players find there.

### Arc III - The Weight of Knowledge

**Faction:** Guild of Threads

**Inciting incident:** Lena Vor appears at Seyla Vorn's stall while the players are there, or approaches them directly if they already crossed paths with the Guild. She studies the Dome mark on the Travelers with intense academic interest and says only: "You need to come to Vel'Ossian. Not now. But before it is too late."

**Objective 1:** Build trust with the Guild through field work.  
Via `guild_01` and `guild_02`.

**Decision point A:** During the Fissure mission, a magical event reveals that the Fissure is a sealing anchor and it is failing. Do the players report it to the Guild or try to repair it on their own?

**Objective 2:** Go to Vel'Ossian and gain access to the third floor.  
Via `guild_03`.

**Decision point B:** The Aeridian Manuscript reveals that the Last Chamber responds to intent. What the players desire when they enter will shape the outcome. Master Sorel asks them to reflect before going. What do they actually want?

**Objective 3:** Decide what they want to happen, and go to the Last Chamber with that clarity.

**Reveal:** The Guild does not intervene because it cannot. The Aeridian Manuscript specifies that the Chamber recognizes only those summoned by the Thread, and the Dome summoned the Travelers. No native of Aerus can access it functionally. The Guild has known this ever since it found the manuscript. That is why it stayed silent: they cannot be the solution. Only the Travelers can.

**Convergence:** The Guild offers the only complete map of the Last Chamber in exchange for the players carrying the Crystal of Silence with them. They do not know exactly what it does, only that the manuscript says it belongs there.

### Arc IV - The Fire That Frees

**Faction:** Children of the Broken Thread

**Inciting incident:** Thresh speaks to the players on the morning of their arrival. He had been waiting. Through the Children network he already knew the Dome would summon outsiders. He asks directly: "The Dome lied to you about what must be done. Do you want to know the truth it left out?"

**Objective 1:** Understand the Children's perspective on the Thread and Vor'Athek.  
Via `children_01` and `children_02`.

**Decision point A:** After hearing Aveth say of Vor'Athek that "it waits for something," do the players investigate what that means or dismiss it?

**Objective 2:** Take part in the ritual at the Pale Belt and choose whether to amplify it or contain it.  
Via `children_03`.

**Decision point B:** If they contain the Surge, Thresh accepts the loss but the Weaver appears and says, "You were right. Now listen to what the Dome never told you." If they amplify it, the Surge forces an unexpected alliance and every faction must sit at the same table.

**Objective 3:** Discover what Vor'Athek actually wants, through Aveth's memories and what the Weaver knows.

**Reveal:** Vor'Athek is not trying to escape the Sealing. It is trying to complete it. The original ritual had a second stage the Aeridians never executed before they died. What everyone calls the collapse of the Sealing is actually Vor'Athek approaching the point where that second stage could be triggered from the inside out. The Last Chamber is where that second stage belongs. If someone enters with the intention of sealing Vor'Athek permanently from the outside, it may use that act to complete the second stage from within. The outcome would be unpredictable.

**Convergence:** Thresh and the Children escort the players to the edge of the Pale Belt. They know the path to the Last Chamber better than any other faction. In exchange, they ask for only one thing: "When you get there, ask it what it wants before you decide anything."

## Convergence - The Last Chamber

### What Each Faction Knows

| Faction | What it knows about the Last Chamber | What it lies about or omits |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Church** | It exists, has a partial map, and may hold the power to "purify the Thread" | Omits that the founder knew about the ritual's second stage |
| **Empire** | The Kael Archive estimated its coordinates and sees it as the Sealing's core | Omits that Valdek IV accelerates the collapse |
| **Guild** | Has the complete map, knows it responds to intent, and knows only Travelers can access it | Omits that the Crystal of Silence belongs there and what it may do |
| **Children** | Know the physical route through Aveth and the Weaver, and believe direct confrontation with Vor'Athek is possible | Thresh omits that even he does not know what the result would be |
| **The Weaver** | Knows the entire truth, including the second stage of the ritual | Omits why she chose not to complete the Sealing when she had the chance |

### The Final Choice

When the Travelers reach the Last Chamber, they face four permanent options for the future of Aerus:

1. **Seal Vor'Athek from the outside.** This is the Dome's apparent objective, but the result is uncertain.
2. **Complete the original ritual.** This requires the Crystal of Silence. It may restore the Thread, but also free Vor'Athek in a controlled way.
3. **Ask Vor'Athek what it wants.** This is the Children's proposal and implies negotiating with a pre-linguistic entity.
4. **Leave without acting.** The Chamber closes, the Sealing continues degrading, and within 20 to 40 years Vor'Athek emerges anyway, with no chance of negotiation.

> GM note: there is no correct answer. The campaign is about the Travelers' choices, not about a predefined "right" ending. The Dome never said what should be done, only where the Travelers had to go. That omission was intentional.
