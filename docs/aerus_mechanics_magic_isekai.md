## VI. Magic System

### Primordial Foundations

Water, Earth, Fire, Air, and Energy/Matter form the five elemental bases. Spirit magic operates separately and primarily scales through Charisma.

### Magic Levels (1 to 10)

Mana cost scales exponentially. Level 10 magic is restricted to characters at level 100.

### Mastery and Alchemical Fusions

Elemental proficiency ranges from level 1 to 20. Fusions require simultaneous mastery across multiple elements:

| Rank | Fusion | Requirement |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Ice, Magma | Level 3 in both base elements |
| 2 | Advanced Ice, Advanced Magma | Level 5 in both base elements |
| 5 | Primordial Chaos | Level 20 in all five elements |

### Aeridian Fragments

Aeridian Fragments are pre-catastrophe artifacts immune to corruption. Within a 50-meter radius, magic becomes completely stable. Destroying a Fragment releases energy equivalent to a maximum-grade Surge. The Empire controls roughly 60% of all known Fragments.

### Flame Seals

The Church's licensing system for legal magic use. Without a Seal, using magic in imperial territory is treated as heresy. Cost varies by region and permitted magic level. Kethara never adopted the system, and Myr accepts Seals but does not require them.

## VII. The Travelers - Isekai Mechanics

### Model: Log Horizon

Travelers do not occupy parallel bodies. There is no immediate return. The character **is** that body now. Death is real death.

**Rooting** describes progressive integration into the Thread of Aerus:

| Period | Sign |
| --- | --- |
| 0-180 days | Occasional dreams in fragmented Aeridian |
| 180-540 days | The Thread becomes perceptible as atmospheric pressure |
| 540-1,095 days | Emotional resonance with Surges; origin memories begin to blur |
| 1,095-1,825 days | Intuitions about the Thread no native should possess |
| 1,825+ days | Complete rooting; Aerus becomes the only home |

A path back does exist, but only after Vor'Athek is fully sealed. Rooted Travelers who attempt to leave earlier dissolve into the Thread.

### Immediate Magical Integration

Travelers arrive with magical sensitivity active from the first moment. That sensitivity is a consequence of passing through the Thread, not a gift from the Dome. They learn magic faster than natives, but are also more vulnerable to the corrupted Thread because they did not build resistance gradually.

### Faction Briefing

Each Traveler receives a faction-specific arrival memory. It is partial, biased, and all they possess on day one.

**Church of the Pure Flame:**  
*"Aerus was corrupted by the arrogance of the ancients. Travelers are sent by Providence to help complete that purification. Distrust anyone who calls the Thread 'corrupted' - they still do not understand what they saw."*

**Empire of Valdrek:**  
*"Aerus is a world under reconstruction. Aeridian Fragments are the key to stability. Travelers arrive with abilities the Empire can use. The Empire rewards loyalty. Distrust anyone who questions the need for control."*

**Guild of Threads:**  
*"The Primordial Thread is corrupted and deteriorating. That is not opinion - it is measurable data. Travelers arrive without an imprint in the Thread. The Guild needs agents without signatures. The work is urgent. Distrust simple explanations - this world has none."*

**Children of the Broken Thread:**  
*"The world is dying, and the factions that should save it prefer to control it. The Last Chamber exists. Travelers are proof that something beyond this world still cares about Aerus. Distrust anyone who says forbidden magic is too dangerous."*

> Every briefing contains one truth, one half-truth, and one calculated omission. The player does not know which is which.

### How NPCs React to the Mark

| Region | Reaction to the Mark |
| --- | --- |
| Port Myr | "Traveler surcharge: +15%. Nothing personal." |
| Urban Valdoria | Double document checks. Flame Seal required. |
| Rural Valdoria | Superstitious responses, either lucky or ominous depending on local folklore. |
| Shaleth | Silent observation. Elves register the fact but rarely approach first. |
| Estravar | Mostly indifferent. Work and Keth matter more than origin. |
| Khorrath | The Order of Cinders treats Marks as evidence tied to Vor'Athek. |
| Veth | Intent-verification ritual required before any formal interaction. |

Common NPC lines:

- "Roll your sleeve up, or I will ask anyway."
- "Traveler. Good. You people usually die fast or last long. Rarely the middle ground."
- "Your Mark is pulsing. There is magic nearby you still haven't seen."
- "Do not ask me what the Dome wants from you. It never answered me either."

### Starting Items - Complete Structure

Every Traveler begins with three groups of items:

**Set 1 - Racial items**  
One combat item, one utility item, and one racial identity item.

**Set 2 - Class items**  
One specialized combat item and one class utility item.

**Set 3 - Dome item**  
Appears on the character's body when they awaken in Aerus, without explanation.

| Archetype | Dome item | Hidden property |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Warrior | Ether Whetstone - +1 damage for 1 hour after 10 minutes of sharpening | Heats up when a Surge is imminent |
| Mage | Thread Notebook - automatically records any observed spell | Ink fades from pages describing spells the Dome does not want learned yet |
| Archer / Explorer | Broken Compass - points toward the greatest magical value within 200 meters | Sometimes points to places where something once was, but is now gone |
| Diplomat / Social | Double Myr Coin - always lands on the side the bearer unconsciously wants | The Dome can force the opposite result once, and the bearer never knows when |
| Healer | White Mist Flask - heals 2d6 VIT, 3 charges, regains one on each new moon | Does not work on injuries caused by the bearer |
| Survivor | Colorless Cloak - adapts its appearance to the environment after 1 hour of use | Sometimes reveals impossible colors, signaling corrupted Thread nearby |
